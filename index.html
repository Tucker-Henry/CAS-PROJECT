About Us

Our Mission

The goal of this project is to raise awareness for the importance of pedestrian safety and how pedestrian safety can be at risk if crosswalks aren’t properly maintained. We also aim to advocate for improving crosswalks in St. Petersburg by adding lights to increase visibility. As of 2021, “Pinellas County has the highest percentage of pedestrian-involved crashes and fatalities in the state” (bay news 9). Pedestrian safety is a vital part of keeping our roads and transportation safe and equitable for everyone’s use. We hope that you can contribute to this project by signing our petition, letting us interview you if you’ve been impacted by unsafe crosswalks, or sharing our message! Thank you for helping us keep Pinellas safe for pedestrians!

Sign the Petition

Help us raise awareness and show the St. Petersburg government that our community cares about safer, well‑lit crosswalks. If you are under 18, you must have a parent or guardian’s permission to sign.

Petition Text
To the Complete Streets and PS&I Committees of the St. Petersburg Government: According to Bay News Nine, as of 2021, “Pinellas County has the highest percentage of pedestrian-involved crashes and fatalities in the state.” Pedestrians are an essential part of daily life in St. Petersburg—students walking to school, families crossing busy roads, and residents moving through their neighborhoods. Ensuring their safety is a responsibility we all share. One member of our group, Tucker Henry, was lightly struck by a car while using a crosswalk that lacked proper lighting. The driver simply could not see him. This incident reflects a broader issue: many crosswalks in our city are poorly lit, reducing visibility and increasing the risk of accidents. Installing lighting at more crosswalks is a simple, effective step that can prevent injuries and save lives. We believe several specific locations would immediately benefit from improved lighting, including: • The crosswalk on 38th Avenue between 5th and 7th Street • The crosswalk on 30th Avenue near 40th Street The Public Services & Infrastructure Committee’s mission includes addressing “public safety… public utilities [and] city-owned development.” We respectfully ask that you prioritize installing lighting at these crosswalks to improve safety for all residents. The Complete Streets Committee’s mission includes ensuring that “new street operations are safe and convenient [for those who are] walking.” We ask that you consider establishing a policy requiring new crosswalks to include lighting as a standard safety feature. To our neighbors, classmates, families, and community members: your voice matters. By signing this petition, you help show our city leaders that this issue affects real people in real neighborhoods every day. Your support raises awareness, strengthens our request, and brings us closer to safer streets for everyone in St. Petersburg. Thank you for your time and commitment to our community.
